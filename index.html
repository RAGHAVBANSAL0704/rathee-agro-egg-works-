How We Work

Our Manufacturing Process

From your initial call to final delivery — a clear, transparent process that ensures your trolley is built right, first time.

💬

Requirement Discussion

We start by understanding your needs — trolley size, load capacity, purpose (grain, sugarcane, sand), and budget. Call or visit our workshop.

📐

Design & Measurement

Our team creates detailed measurements and designs based on your specifications. We ensure compatibility with your tractor make and model.

🔩

Material Selection

Premium MS steel and components are selected to match the job requirements — thickness, grade, and specifications are chosen for durability.

🏗️

Fabrication

Our workshop cuts, bends, and shapes the steel into the frame and body components. Every cut is measured twice, made once.

⚡

Welding & Assembly

Expert welders assemble the trolley with full-penetration welds at every joint — no shortcuts. Axles, wheels, draw-bars all mounted and aligned.

🔍

Quality Inspection

Every completed trolley undergoes a thorough inspection — load testing, weld inspection, wheel alignment, and paint quality check.

🚛

Delivery

Your trolley is delivered to your location or collected from our workshop. We provide basic usage guidance and warranty on our workmanship.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often. Can't find what you need? Just call us.

Do you manufacture custom-size trolleys?
+
Yes, absolutely! Custom trolleys are one of our specialties. We can build trolleys to any length, width, or load capacity. Just share your requirements — tractor model, intended load, road type — and we'll design and fabricate to your specifications. Custom jobs take 7–15 days depending on complexity.
How long does trolley repair work take?
+
Minor repairs (welding cracks, fixing a side board, replacing bolts) are usually done same-day or within 24 hours. Major structural repairs — frame straightening, axle replacement, complete body overhaul — typically take 3–7 days. We'll give you a time estimate when we inspect the trolley.
What materials do you use in trolley manufacturing?
+
We use high-tensile Mild Steel (MS) for all our trolley frames, floors, and body panels. The thickness and grade depends on the required capacity — typically IS 2062 grade steel. Axles use heavy-duty forged steel. All surfaces receive an anti-rust primer coat followed by industrial enamel paint for weather protection.
Do you provide delivery of completed trolleys?
+
Yes, we arrange delivery within Rohtak district and nearby areas. For longer distances across Haryana or other states, we can coordinate transport at actual transport cost. Alternatively, customers are welcome to collect from our Lakhan Majra workshop. Please discuss delivery at the time of order.
Can tractor bumpers be customized with names or designs?
+
Yes! Many customers get their name, village name, or tractor number engraved or embossed on the bumper. We can also create decorative cutouts and patterns. Just let us know your preference at the time of order. This usually adds 1–2 days to the manufacturing time.
What is the price of a standard tractor trolley?
+
Pricing depends on size, capacity, steel thickness, and features. A standard 10-ft trolley body starts from a competitive price that we quote based on current steel rates. We provide transparent, itemized quotes — no hidden charges. Call or WhatsApp us with your requirements for an instant estimate.
Do you provide any warranty on your trolleys?
+
Yes, we stand behind our workmanship. We offer a warranty on our welding and fabrication work. Any structural defect arising from our manufacturing will be repaired at no charge. Normal wear, overloading damage, or accidental damage is not covered. Please discuss warranty terms at the time of purchase.
How do I get a price estimate for my requirement?
+
The quickest way is to call us or WhatsApp with your requirement details (trolley size, capacity, tractor model for bumpers, etc.). You can also fill the contact form on this page. We respond within a few hours and can often give you a ballpark estimate immediately.
